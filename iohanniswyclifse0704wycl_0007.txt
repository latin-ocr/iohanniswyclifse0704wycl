INTRODUCTION.

1. The Contents.

The present volume completes the great collection of Wyclif’s
La'tin Sermons.

Their importance has been sufficiently appreciated in the intro-
duction to the first three volumes.

It is recognized that there is no part of Wyclif’s activity as a
reformes which has not been treated of in his sermons, and that
in an easy, attractive and popular manner, free from the scholastic
baggagewhich encumbers his other works.

We might then refrain from attempting to give a general view
of the contents of the present volume, were it not necessary to insist
on one circumstance which if overlooked may lead to misunderstanding
and error, and may cause the Reformer to be charged with contra-
dictions and equivocations.

this volume will find many passages which are in direct contradiction
to occasional assertions in other sermons also in this volume.
